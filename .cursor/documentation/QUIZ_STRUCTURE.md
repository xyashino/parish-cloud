# Quiz Structure - Romantic App

## Overview

The quiz consists of different question categories that help couples get to know and understand each other better. Each category focuses on a different aspect of the relationship, from light topics to deeper, more intimate areas.

## Question Format

Each question contains:

- **Question text** - clearly formulated, understandable
- **Answer type** - multiple choice, scale (1-5), yes/no, open text
- **Time to answer** - from 30 seconds to 2 minutes depending on difficulty
- **Difficulty level** - easy, medium, hard
- **Educational tip** - brief explanation after answering (optional)

## Question Categories

### 1. Relationship Dynamics (What happens in the relationship)

**Goal:** Understanding relationship dynamics, daily interactions, and communication patterns.

**Sample questions:**

**Easy level:**

- How often during the week do you spend time together just the two of you? (A) Daily (B) 3-4 times (C) 1-2 times (D) Rarely
- When was the last time you did something nice for your partner without a reason? (Open text, max 100 characters)
- How important is it for you that your partner remembers important dates (anniversaries, birthdays)? (Scale 1-5)

**Medium level:**

- How do you handle it when your partner is in a bad mood? (A) I try to cheer them up (B) I give them space (C) I talk about the problem (D) I wait until it passes
- What is most important to you in daily communication with your partner? (Multiple choice: honesty, humor, support, intimacy, independence)
- How often do you argue about everyday matters (cleaning, shopping, plans)? (Scale 1-5, where 1 = never, 5 = very often)

**Hard level:**

- Describe a situation where you felt misunderstood by your partner. What did you do then? (Open text, max 200 characters)
- What are your biggest concerns about the future of your relationship? (Multiple choice: routine, infidelity, loss of feelings, financial problems, differences in values)
- What is most difficult for you in compromises with your partner? (Open text)

### 2. Self-Discovery

**Goal:** Helping to discover one's own needs, values, emotions, and behavioral patterns.

**Sample questions:**

**Easy level:**

- How do you best relax after a hard day? (A) Solitude and silence (B) Talking with loved ones (C) Physical activity (D) Entertainment (movie, book)
- What motivates you most in life? (Multiple choice: career success, relationships, personal development, adventures, stability)
- How often do you analyze your emotions and feelings? (Scale 1-5)

**Medium level:**

- What are your main life values? Choose 3 most important. (Multiple choice: family, career, freedom, security, adventure, development, helping others, creativity)
- How do you react to stress? (A) I seek support from others (B) I withdraw into myself (C) I act actively (D) I avoid the problem
- What is most difficult for you in expressing emotions? (Multiple choice: fear of judgment, lack of words, shame, lack of trust, inability to recognize emotions)

**Hard level:**

- Describe a moment when you changed the most as a person. What was it? (Open text, max 200 characters)
- What are your biggest fears and how do they affect your relationship? (Open text)
- What would you like to change about yourself but have difficulty with? (Open text)

---

### 3. Sexual Intimacy

**Goal:** Open conversation about needs, boundaries, preferences, and intimacy in the relationship.

**Sample questions:**

**Easy level:**

- How important is physical intimacy to you in a relationship? (Scale 1-5)
- How often would you like to have intimate contact with your partner? (A) Daily (B) Several times a week (C) Once a week (D) Less often
- What is most important to you in intimacy? (Multiple choice: frequency, quality, emotional closeness, experimentation, safety)

**Medium level:**

- How do you communicate your intimate needs to your partner? (A) I talk directly (B) I give subtle signals (C) I wait for the partner to initiate (D) I rarely talk about it
- What are your boundaries in intimacy? (Open text, max 150 characters)
- What makes you feel most desired by your partner? (Multiple choice: compliments, touch, time together, partner's initiative, communication)

**Hard level:**

- Are there topics related to intimacy that are difficult for you to talk about? What are they? (Open text)
- How do you handle it when your intimate needs differ from your partner's? (Open text)
- What would you like to try in intimacy but are afraid to talk about? (Open text, optional)

### 4. Mutual Respect

**Goal:** Understanding how partners show respect to each other and what behaviors are important to them.

**Sample questions:**

**Easy level:**

- How important is it for you that your partner respects your opinion, even when they disagree? (Scale 1-5)
- What is a sign of respect to you in a relationship? (Multiple choice: listening, keeping promises, respect for time, recognition of achievements, respect for boundaries)
- How do you react when your partner disagrees with your opinion? (A) I respect the difference of opinion (B) I try to convince them (C) I get upset (D) I avoid the topic)

**Medium level:**

- How important is it for you that your partner respects your personal space and time for yourself? (Scale 1-5)
- What is the biggest lack of respect to you in a relationship? (Multiple choice: lies, ignoring, criticizing in front of others, not keeping promises, violating boundaries)
- How do you show respect to your partner on a daily basis? (Open text, max 150 characters)

**Hard level:**

- Describe a situation where you felt disrespected by your partner. What happened? (Open text)
- How do you handle differences in values between you and your partner? (Open text)
- What is most difficult for you in showing respect to your partner when you disagree with them? (Open text)

### 5. Communication and Conflicts

**Goal:** Understanding communication styles and ways of handling conflicts.

**Sample questions:**

**Easy level:**

- How often do you talk about your feelings? (A) Daily (B) Several times a week (C) Once a week (D) Rarely
- What is most difficult for you in communication with your partner? (Multiple choice: expressing emotions, listening, avoiding arguments, understanding partner's perspective)
- How do you react during an argument? (A) I stay and talk (B) I need a break (C) I raise my voice (D) I withdraw)

**Medium level:**

- What are your most common topics of arguments with your partner? (Multiple choice: finances, time, household duties, family, friends, future)
- What helps you calm down during a conflict? (Multiple choice: time alone, conversation, physical activity, music, meditation)
- How important is it for you to always tell the truth, even if it might hurt? (Scale 1-5)

**Hard level:**

- Describe the most difficult argument you had with your partner. What was the cause and how did you resolve it? (Open text)
- Are there topics you avoid discussing with your partner? What are they and why? (Open text)
- How do you handle emotions when your partner criticizes you? (Open text)

---

### 6. Values and Life Goals

**Goal:** Discovering shared values, goals, and vision for the future.

**Sample questions:**

**Easy level:**

- How important is your professional career to you? (Scale 1-5)
- Would you like to have children in the future? (A) Yes, definitely (B) Maybe (C) No (D) I don't know yet
- What is most important to you in life? Choose 3. (Multiple choice: family, career, health, adventures, financial stability, personal development, helping others)

**Medium level:**

- How do you imagine an ideal relationship in 5 years? (Open text, max 200 characters)
- How important is it for you that your partner shares your values? (Scale 1-5)
- What is most important to you in planning the future with your partner? (Multiple choice: shared goals, independence, stability, flexibility, development)

**Hard level:**

- What are your biggest life dreams and how can your partner participate in them? (Open text)
- Are there values that are non-negotiable for you in a relationship? What are they? (Open text)
- How do you handle it when your life goals differ from your partner's? (Open text)

---

### 7. Love Languages and Showing Affection

**Goal:** Understanding how partners express and receive love.

**Sample questions:**

**Easy level:**

- How do you most often show love to your partner? (Multiple choice: words, touch, gifts, acts, time together)
- What makes you feel most loved? (Multiple choice: compliments, hugging, surprises, help in daily life, time together)
- How often do you tell your partner "I love you"? (A) Daily (B) Several times a week (C) Rarely (D) Almost never)

**Medium level:**

- Does your way of showing love differ from what you need? (Yes/No + Open text)
- What is most important to you in showing affection? (Multiple choice: spontaneity, regularity, honesty, creativity, simplicity)
- How do you react when your partner doesn't show affection in the way you need? (Open text)

**Hard level:**

- Describe a moment when you felt most loved by your partner. What happened then? (Open text)
- Are there ways of showing love that are difficult for you? What are they and why? (Open text)
- How has your way of showing love changed since the beginning of the relationship? (Open text)

---

### 8. Trust and Fidelity

**Goal:** Conversation about trust, fidelity, and boundaries in the relationship.

**Sample questions:**

**Easy level:**

- How important is trust to you in a relationship? (Scale 1-5)
- Can your partner have friends of the opposite sex? (A) Yes, no problem (B) Yes, but with certain boundaries (C) No, that's a problem for me (D) I have no opinion)
- How often do you check your partner's phone/messaging apps? (A) Never (B) Rarely (C) Sometimes (D) Often)

**Medium level:**

- What is a sign of trust to you in a relationship? (Multiple choice: access to phone, keeping promises, honesty, independence, lack of control)
- How do you define infidelity? (Multiple choice: physical contact, emotional closeness with another person, lies, hiding information, flirting)
- What would you do if you found out your partner lied to you about something important? (Open text)

**Hard level:**

- Have you had experiences with lack of trust in previous relationships? How do they affect your current relationship? (Open text)
- What are your boundaries regarding contact with other people? (Open text)
- What is most difficult for you in building trust? (Open text)

## Quiz Mechanics

### Session Structure

1. **Category selection** - Players choose a category to start
2. **Question randomization** - System randomly selects questions from the chosen category (mix of difficulty levels)
3. **Time to answer** - Each question has a time limit (30s - 2min)
4. **Simultaneous answers** - Both players answer simultaneously without seeing the partner's answer
5. **Results reveal** - After both players answer, results and comparison are shown
6. **Educational tips** - Optional explanations and advice after the question
7. **Category summary** - After completing a category, compatibility statistics are shown

### Scoring System

- **Answer compatibility** - Points for similar answers
- **Difficulty level** - More points for harder questions
- **Honesty bonus** - Bonus for answering difficult questions
- **Completeness** - Bonus for completing the entire category

### Additional Features

- **Skip question** - Ability to skip a question that is too difficult
- **Save for later** - Marking a question to return to
- **Answer history** - Ability to view previous answers
- **Time comparison** - How answers changed over time

## Implementation Recommendations

### Question Database

- Minimum 20-30 questions per category
- Mix of answer types (multiple choice, scale, open text)
- Difficulty levels evenly distributed

### UX/UI

- Friendly, non-intimidating interface
- Ability to pause at any time
- Warnings before difficult questions
- Option for answer anonymity

### Security

- Privacy option - who can see answers
- Ability to delete answers
- Warnings before sensitive topics
